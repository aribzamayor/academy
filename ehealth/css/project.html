BANANA ISLAND

Banana Island, which is Nigeria’sanswer to Paris’s Seventh Arrondissement,
San Diego’s La Jolla, New York City’s Tribeca and Tokyo’s Shibuya and Roppongi,
is a small man-made island in the Ikoyi neighborhood of Lagos.
Completed in 2000, it’s a billionaires’ paradise populated by the country’s
richest and most well-known families, who savor its quiet, peaceful atmosphere
far from the clamor and crowds of Lagos, the country’s largest city and financial center.
Its name comes from its distinct shape.

VICTORIA ISLAND


Victoria Island is the beauty of Lagos city. The neighborhood is so well known that even
the names of its streets have become iconic across the nation. This island is only one of Lagos’
districts that has the most inimitable skyline.
Originally surrounded entirely by water, It was bordered by the Atlantic ocean on the south, the mouth
of the Lagos Lagoon on the West, the Five Cowrie Creek to the north North, and swamps on the East.
It has since, however, been filled (by the British colonial government to reduce mosquito breeding areas),
creating land bridge between Victoria Island and Lekki Peninsula, and ending its existence as a true island.
